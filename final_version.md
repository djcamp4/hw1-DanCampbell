AI for Social Justice

I am excited about the potential for artificial intelligence to advance social justice. I believe that AI, if developed and applied fairly, can help address some of the deep-rooted inequities that persist in our society. Too often, the systems we rely on for critical decisions carry forward historical biases rather than correcting them. Generative AI and related technologies offer a real opportunity to change that, but only if we are intentional about how we build and deploy them.

1) Fairer credit and lending decisions:** AI models can be designed to evaluate creditworthiness using a broader and more equitable set of factors than the traditional FICO score, which has historically disadvantaged minority and low-income communities. By rethinking how we assess financial risk, AI could help expand access to loans and homeownership for underserved populations.
2) Enabling a shorter work week and universal basic income:** As AI systems take on more routine and repetitive tasks, they have the potential to dramatically reduce the amount of labor required to sustain our economy. This could pave the way for policies like universal basic income (UBI), giving people the freedom to work less and instead pursue their passions, education, and personal growth.
3) Making education more accessible:** Generative AI can serve as a personalized tutor, making high-quality educational support available to anyone with an internet connection regardless of their socioeconomic background. From adaptive learning tools to real-time language translation, AI can help break down the barriers that keep education out of reach for many.
